Synthetic Heparin

Heparin is a commonly used anticoagulant drug in hospitals with a market size of $ 3-4 billion annually. Currently, heparin is isolated from porcine intestine through a long and poorly regulated supply chain. In 2007, batches of contaminated heparin entered the US market, killing more than 200 patients in the US. The contamination accident revealed the vulnerability of the heparin supply chain. Glycan Therapeutics is using a highly efficient chemoenzymatic approach to make synthetic heparin to substitute the animal sourced heparin product. Our product will be animal- free, eliminating the contamination risk. Synthetic heparin also display additional pharmacological advantages: safe for renal-impaired patients and its anticoagulant activity can be reversed by protamine. Synthetic heparin will be a safer and better anticoagulant drug than heparin in the future.
